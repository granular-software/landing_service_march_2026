Why they hit structural limits

A native Pipedrive agent can only reason inside Pipedrive.

  • Operational constraints What Pipedrive may not know

    A deal might depend on an unresolved support escalation, an unpaid invoice, onboarding capacity next month, a contract condition, or an internal approval rule.

    None of these constraints necessarily live inside Pipedrive, even when they determine what should happen next.

  • Architectural consequence Where fragility begins

    Once the decision depends on multiple systems, the agent stops operating on reality and starts operating on a partial model of it.

    And that is exactly where fragile automation begins.

Implementation path

How Granular turns a Pipedrive entry point into a working system.

Pipedrive is where the conversation starts. Granular builds the system underneath that lets the agent reason across the business.

01
Map the business

Build the map around Pipedrive.

We map the entities, rules, and constraints around Pipedrive: support load, billing state, onboarding capacity, and approval paths.

Result: the agent reasons on a business map, not a single product record.
02
Connect systems

Connect the systems and mirror permissions.

We connect the systems that actually shape decisions: support, billing, and internal tools. Permissions are mirrored so the agent cannot act outside a user's scope.

Result: the agent sees and acts within the same boundaries as the employee using it.
03
Constrain actions first

Start with a tightly constrained action surface.

The map can be broad from day one. What stays narrow at first is the agent's right to act. We start with strict rules, validation steps, and a limited set of allowed actions. As reliability is observed, that action surface expands.

Result: autonomy grows from observed reliability, not from optimistic prompting.
